1. Executive Summary




VentureOS is a full-stack business intelligence and product-sourcing platform targeting Bangladeshi resellers, importers, and SME owners. It combines AI-powered market analysis, real-time product sourcing across Chinese and local platforms, geolocation competitor intelligence, shipping cost comparison, and business performance tracking — all in one unified workspace with both Bengali and English interfaces.


Dimension
	Summary
	Problem
	Bangladeshi resellers and SME owners lack a unified tool to find trending products, validate market demand, compare sourcing costs, and track business performance.
	Solution
	VentureOS: one platform to research → source → plan → track — built specifically for the Bangladesh market with local payment, language, and regulatory context.
	Primary Users
	Resellers/importers (product-first) and SME owners (business-first) — both user modes supported from Day 1.
	Revenue Model
	Freemium — free tier with core features; paid plans unlock AI blueprint generation, advanced analytics, and team collaboration.
	AI Strategy
	Hybrid: Ollama + Qwen2.5-7b for fast local tasks; Claude API for blueprints, market analysis, and complex reasoning.
	Launch Language
	Bengali + English simultaneously from Day 1.
	MVP Timeline
	3 months to first working product; full platform by Month 9.
	Total Modules
	12 feature modules (including 2 new AI database and automation modules).
	

★  NORTH STAR: A reseller in Dhaka can open VentureOS, type a product idea in Bengali, and within 60 seconds receive: market demand data, competitor analysis, supplier links, shipping costs, projected profit margins, and a content strategy — without leaving the platform.

2. Problem Statement & Product Vision


2.1 The Problem
Bangladeshi entrepreneurs and resellers face a fragmented, high-risk workflow today:
* No unified tool to discover trending products from China or global markets
* No way to validate local market demand before investing capital
* Sourcing platforms (Alibaba, Pinduoduo, Xianyu) are in Chinese — language barrier
* Shipping cost comparison across agencies is fully manual
* Business tracking is done in spreadsheets or not at all
* No localized blueprint generator that understands NBR tax, bKash, Eid cycles
* No structured AI database of where to research products or which sites launch products first


2.2 Product Vision
VentureOS becomes the single operating system for any Bangladesh-based entrepreneur — from the moment they think of a product idea to the moment they are tracking live sales performance.


3. Target Users & User Modes


VentureOS serves two primary user types through two onboarding modes:


Mode A — Product Reseller / Importer
	Mode B — SME Owner / Existing Business
	Starts with a product idea or category. Wants to find what to sell, where to source it, how much to ship, and whether demand exists locally.Key inputs: product idea, budget, location, warehouse capacity, target accounts (domestic/international)
	Has an existing business. Wants to optimise operations, understand market position, track financial performance, and find adjacent products to expand.Key inputs: business type, team size, current revenue, categories, goals

6. Feature Modules — Complete Specification


VentureOS is organized into 12 feature modules. All modules are planned from Day 1 and delivered in phases. Modules 1–10 are user-facing; Module 11 is a server-side AI research database; Module 12 is a developer-only automation dataset.


Module 1 — AI Onboarding Engine
	MVP — Month 1–3
	

The entry point to VentureOS. Collects structured user context through a conversational interface and routes the user to Mode A (reseller) or Mode B (SME owner). Powered by a local AI model running on-device — no internet required.


1.1 Onboarding Input Fields
Input Field
	What It Means & Why It Matters
	Geographical Location
	City, district, or region. Used to pull local market data, competitor density, and customer demand heatmaps.
	Product Idea / Type
	The item or service the user wants to sell. Triggers category-specific analysis and sourcing suggestions.
	Business Type
	Reseller, manufacturer, service provider, exporter, etc. Determines which blueprint template and financial model is used.
	Total Investment
	How much money is available to start. Sets realistic revenue projections and limits strategy to what is affordable.
	Team Size
	Number of people working in the business. Affects role recommendations, workload planning, and collaboration features.
	Warehouse / Storage Capacity
	Available storage space in sq ft or number of units. Influences inventory planning and reorder suggestions.
	Account Type
	Domestic only or international (import/export). Unlocks cross-border features and export readiness scoring.
	

★  Smart follow-up: If the user selects international accounts, the system automatically asks about target countries, preferred currencies, and export documentation status.

10.1 What Is Pre-Data?
Pre-Data is structured market research collected automatically before the user commits to a product. It answers three questions: Is this product available? Where is it cheapest? And what do buyers say about it?

11.3 Auto-Update System
The database updates automatically via Celery Beat scheduled jobs. The update process runs on a tiered schedule:


Update Tier
	Frequency
	Trigger
	What Updates
	Tier 1 — High-frequency sites
	Every 6 hours
	Celery Beat schedule
	follower_count, active status, new products detected flag
	Tier 2 — Standard sites
	Daily at 03:00 UTC
	Celery Beat schedule
	All fields including launch_priority_score recalculation
	Tier 3 — Low-frequency sites
	Weekly on Monday
	Celery Beat schedule
	Full record refresh and AI enrichment pass
	Tier 4 — Manual / New sites
	On-demand
	Admin trigger or AI discovery
	New site record creation and initial scoring
	

* Every update creates a new version record in the site_versions table — full audit trail maintained indefinitely
* Changed fields are recorded per version so diffs can be computed between any two versions
* If a site is detected as gone/404 for 7+ days, active is set to false — record is NOT deleted
* AI enrichment pass (Qwen2.5-7b locally) re-scores launch_priority_score weekly using recent first-launch detection data
* New sites are discovered automatically when scraping identifies a new page/store in a tracked category


11.4 How AI Uses This Database
When a user or internal process triggers a product research task, the AI Router queries this database as the first step:
1. User submits a product idea (e.g., "portable blenders")
2. AI Router maps the idea to a product category (e.g., "kitchen_appliances")
3. Query category_launch_leaders to get top 5 sites ranked by launch_priority_score for that category
4. Query marketplace_sites to get active sites in that category with their platform_type and URLs
5. AI research agent (Playwright + Claude API) visits top-ranked sites first to detect new products, prices, and trends
6. Results fed into Module 10 (Pre-Data Intelligence) and Module 4 (Blueprint Generator)


★  The Marketplace Intelligence Database is the memory layer that makes AI research faster and more targeted. Instead of searching the entire internet, the AI knows exactly where to look — and which sources launch products first in each category.

Module 12 — Product Research Site Dataset
	MVP Internal — Developer Only
	

⚠  DEVELOPER-ONLY MODULE — This dataset is NOT exposed to end users and is NOT part of any user-facing API. Access is restricted to internal development team members only. Do NOT include in public API documentation or user-facing admin panels.
	

12.1 Purpose & Overview
Module 12 is a small, curated internal dataset that defines which websites, platforms, and online marketplaces the VentureOS automation system is authorized to use for product research. It acts as an allowlist and configuration layer for the scraping and AI research automation — controlling WHERE research happens, HOW it happens, and under what constraints.


This dataset is separate from Module 11 (which is a live, auto-updating database of all discovered sites). Module 12 is a static-to-semi-static configuration dataset that developers maintain and review. It enforces ethical scraping boundaries, rate limits, and legal compliance rules per site.


12.2 Why This Dataset Exists
* Not all sites should be scraped — some have ToS that prohibit automated access
* Different sites require different scraping strategies, rate limits, and authentication approaches
* Developers need a single source of truth for which sites automation is allowed to access
* Compliance and legal review requires knowing exactly which external sites the system touches
* Automation reliability: knowing which sites are stable vs. fragile helps prioritize maintenance

12.6 Automation Workflow — How This Dataset Is Used
7. Scraping scheduler (Celery Beat) fires a research job for a product category
8. AI Router queries research_sites WHERE scraping_enabled = true AND health_status = healthy AND primary_category matches OR research_purpose contains the category keyword
9. For each returned site, automation reads: access_method, rate_limit_rpm, requires_proxy, proxy_pool, js_rendering_required to configure the scraper correctly
10. Scraping job runs within the defined rate limits and proxy settings
11. Results written to MongoDB product collection and flagged for AI enrichment
12. health_status and last_health_check updated after each run


⚠  This dataset is the contract between the development team and the automation system. No scraper should access any external site that is not in this dataset with scraping_enabled = true. Any exception requires explicit team lead approval and must be documented in the notes field.
